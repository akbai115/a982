CORE ALLEGATIONS

Per public filings and reporting; allegations only — no findings of liability

Unregistered Securities

Offerings/sales under Securities Act §§5, 12 (Solana-based meme coins treated as investment contracts)

Money Laundering (Lazarus Group)

Alleged facilitation of $1.5 Billion in illicit funds, specifically citing North Korean hackers (Lazarus Group) laundering Bybit exploit proceeds via bonding curves.

RICO Violations

18 U.S.C. §1961 et seq. — Alleged coordinated "Pump Enterprise" involving platform, infrastructure providers, and validators.

Gambling Operations

Unlicensed casino operations; "bonding curve" mechanics described as rigged games of chance rather than trading.

MEV Front-Running

Integration with Jito Labs tools allegedly allowed insiders to front-run retail buys in the same block.

Consumer Protection

Unjust enrichment claims citing >98% user loss rate.

REGULATORY SCRUTINY & OPERATIONAL BLOCKS

UK FCA Interference & Geoblocking

  • FCA Warning: The UK Financial Conduct Authority flagged Pump.fun for unauthorized financial promotions.
  • Action Taken: Pump.fun implemented geoblocking for UK IP addresses, effectively banning British users from the frontend interface to mitigate regulatory risk.

Cease-and-Desist "Impersonation" Drama

  • The Issue: Intense wave of coins impersonating law firms, brands, and individuals.
  • Legal Action: Firms such as Burwick Law and Wolf Popper issued cease-and-desist letters demanding the removal of 200+ impersonating assets.
  • Court Status: While sanctions were initially denied (Jan 23, 2026), the court issued a stern warning that recurring harassment could lead to future penalties.

Broader Global Scrutiny

  • EU MiCA: Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulations pose an existential threat to the anonymous "issuer" model of memecoins.
  • SEC Position: Continued debate on whether algorithmic bonding curves constitute "investment contracts" (Howey Test matches).
